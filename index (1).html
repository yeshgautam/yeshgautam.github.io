Yesh Gautam

/jeːɕ ɡɔːtəm/ — proper noun, Sanskrit origin

यश yaśa
Sanskrit — glory, fame, renown
from the root yaś, "to be honored" — the same root behind the philosophical concept of a life measured by what it leaves behind, not what it accumulates.
गौतम gautama
Sanskrit — patronymic, "descendant of Gotama"
an old gotra name carried by scholars, sages, and, most famously, the Buddha — a lineage tied to teaching and inquiry.

Put together: someone trying to earn a good name through close study — of languages, of cricket scorecards, of whatever's in front of him. I'm a rising sophomore at Eden Prairie High School, currently building BraveVoice, a speech-therapy app that gamifies stuttering exercises for kids, learning Sanskrit and French, and spending the rest of my hours on a cricket pitch or in a rowing shell.
